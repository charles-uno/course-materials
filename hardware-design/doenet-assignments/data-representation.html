This exercise walks you through converting the following IEEE 754 standard floating point binary number to decimal. You are not expected to memorize this process.

10111010100110000010000000000000

The first (left-most) bit is the sign bit. What is the sign bit? 1

Is the number above positive or negative?

Positive Negative

After the sign bit, then next eight bits are the exponent bits, representing an unsigned binary integer. What are these eight bits? 01110101 What is this integer in decimal? 117

The remaining bits are the significand bits. What are these bits? 00110000010000000000000 Imagine there is a binary point before the start of these bits, and convert the resulting binary fixed point number into decimal. Your answer should be between 0 and 1. Answer: 0.1884765625

To put these pieces together, we use:

(-1)^{\textrm{[sign bit]}}\times 2^{\textrm{[converted exponent bits]}} \times 1.\textrm{[converted significand bits]}

Filling in the blanks using your answers above, the computation is (this will not be correct until your answers above are correct):

(-1)^{$sign}\times 2^{$exponent-127} \times 1+$significand

Performing this computation, we get the decimal number: -0.001160621643
